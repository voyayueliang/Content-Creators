# 内容方法原则

These principles generalize prior content workflows into reusable rules for conversation-to-publication work.

## 1. 先查看，不急着写

When the source is long, messy, or boundary-sensitive, start with a content view instead of a full draft.

```markdown
## 内容查看台

- 素材适合做什么：
- 最值得展开的方向：
- 不适合硬写成长文的部分：
- 目标读者：
- 最适合的平台：
- 公开边界：
- 下一步：
```

The goal is to decide before writing:

- Is this a dialogue, essay, video script, short post, or internal asset?
- Which platform should come first?
- Which part is not ready for public release?
- What human confirmation is needed?

## 2. 对谈是一等输出形式

Not every interview, livestream, or podcast should become a deep essay.

Keep the dialogue form when:

- guest wording is stronger than summary
- the host's questions are valuable
- the thought is still forming
- the exchange itself shows how an idea emerged
- synthesis would erase uncertainty, relationship, or texture

Edited dialogue is not a lesser form. It can be more honest than a polished argument.

## 3. AI 是第二张工作台，不是代笔

AI can organize, classify, draft, compare, summarize, and check.

AI should not decide:

- what the publisher truly thinks
- which relationship details may be public
- whether a story should be published
- which claim represents the speaker
- whether a draft sounds like the publishing account

The human remains responsible for judgment, voice, consent, and relationship risk.

## 4. 先有现场，再有观点

Avoid opening with naked trends or abstract concepts.

Prefer this order:

1. real scene, person, work, quote, or tension
2. why this detail matters
3. what question it opens
4. cautious judgment
5. what remains unresolved

This keeps articles grounded and reduces AI味.

## 5. 去 AI 味不是换词

The problem is not only vocabulary. The deeper problem is when AI takes over judgment structure.

High-risk signals:

- the draft is too smooth
- every paragraph has a neat conclusion
- the article starts with a grand era statement
- people become examples too quickly
- stories become frameworks too quickly
- the ending suddenly asks for generic engagement

Repair by returning to material:

- concrete scene
- original question
- speaker texture
- visible uncertainty
- evidence and boundary

## 6. 证据比流畅重要

Every public claim should answer:

- Which line, scene, work, behavior, or feedback supports this?
- Is this fact, paraphrase, interpretation, or speculation?
- Should this be marked as `待确认`?
- Is the wording more certain than the source allows?

Do not make incomplete material look complete.

## 7. 保留“不知道”和“还没想完”

Public content does not need to close every loop.

It is acceptable to keep:

- a question not fully answered
- a tension that remains
- a speaker's hesitation
- a cautious interpretation
- a next question instead of a final slogan

Do not remove all complexity for the sake of readability.

## 8. 四道门

Before publication, pass four gates:

1. **Logic gate**: What is this piece really saying?
2. **Evidence gate**: Which claims are supported?
3. **Voice gate**: Does it sound like the publishing account and the speakers?
4. **Relationship gate**: Has anyone been overexposed, overinterpreted, or used as material?

If one gate fails, revise before publishing.

## 9. 平台适配不能牺牲位置

Platform rewriting can change entry point, length, and format. It cannot change the publisher's position.

- WeChat: field texture, dialogue, reflection, method
- short video: one scene, one question, one spoken line
- short social post: one observation or case, not fake intimacy
- newsletter: context and analysis for a different reader
- internal asset: when public boundary is unclear

Do not expand every platform by default.

## 10. 内容资产不是一次性文章

After publication work, recover assets:

- source card
- speaker card
- scene card
- quote card
- case card
- method card
- boundary card
- feedback card

A good workflow should make the next article, event, product, or conversation easier.

## 11. 自动化只自动化重复劳动

Can automate:

- transcript cleanup
- source map
- title candidates
- output routing
- draft variants
- quality checklists
- asset cards
- feedback sorting

Do not automate:

- whether to publish
- how to evaluate a person
- whether to reveal a story
- which claim represents the publisher
- relationship and consent decisions

## 12. 反馈回收

Publishing is not the end.

After release, collect:

- comments
- private messages
- repost language
- consultation or collaboration signals
- reader questions
- phrases readers repeat
- objections or confusion

Use feedback to update future topics, titles, examples, and public boundaries.
