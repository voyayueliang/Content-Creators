# AI Tone Pattern Dictionary

Use this reference to diagnose why a sentence feels like AI. The goal is to extract reusable judgment, not only change words.

## High-Risk Patterns

### 1. Over-neat Contrast

Signals:

- `不是 A，而是 B`
- `不只 / 不仅 ... 也 / 还 ...`
- `相比停在...我们更想...`

Why it feels like AI:

- It turns a simple point into a staged argument.
- It sounds like a template paragraph, especially in public event copy.

Repair:

- State the concrete point directly.
- Use contrast only when the material truly contains tension.

### 2. Empty Specificity

Signals:

- `很具体的问题`
- `真实场景`
- `长期价值`
- `现场议题很集中`
- `问题会变得朴素很多`

Why it feels like AI:

- The words promise concreteness without naming anything concrete.

Repair:

- Name the actual task, person, setting, object, or decision.

### 3. Over-complete Summary

Signals:

- Every paragraph ends in a neat conclusion.
- The ending suddenly expands into values,时代,生态,未来, or意义.
- A draft keeps telling the reader what the material means before showing it.

Why it feels like AI:

- Real creators often leave some texture, uncertainty, or unfinished judgment.

Repair:

- Let the scene, quote, agenda item, or source material carry part of the meaning.
- End with a smaller, more useful next step.

### 4. List-Like Reporting Disguised as Story

Signals:

- Many consecutive paragraphs use `X 将分享...`, `Y 会讨论...`, `Z 将带来...`.
- Sections follow the same syntax even when topics differ.

Why it feels like AI:

- It reads like an agenda rearranged into paragraphs.

Repair:

- Group items by reader question or decision.
- Vary rhythm: some topics can be named briefly; only the most important need explanation.

### 5. Backstage Editor Language

Signals:

- `第一篇里已经介绍`
- `这篇文章将`
- `我们前面说过`
- Internal validation labels, workflow language, or packaging standards appear in public copy.

Why it feels like AI:

- The reader sees the production process instead of the article.

Repair:

- Write from the publication context, not the drafting context.
- Delete internal process notes unless the article is explicitly about process.

### 6. Generic Momentum

Signals:

- `模型发布之后，开发者的工作才刚开始`
- `这个想象很有吸引力`
- `从演示走向生产环境`
- `从概念验证到真实业务的跨越`

Why it feels like AI:

- These may be true, but they are too portable. They could fit many articles.

Repair:

- Tie the claim to one source material, forum topic, user pain, or real decision.

## Replacement Heuristics

Use this order:

1. What is the actual thing happening?
2. Who is affected?
3. What does the reader need to know or do?
4. What remains uncertain or needs confirmation?

Prefer:

- short direct sentences
- specific nouns
- verbs such as `看`, `问`, `改`, `跑`, `接`, `写`, `确认`
- smaller claims that can be checked

Avoid:

- grand transitions
- universal conclusions
- over-polished value statements
- fake warmth
- repeated sentence frames
