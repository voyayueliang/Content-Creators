# Final Gate Checklist

Use this reference for publication-ready review. Run fact/boundary checks before voice polishing.

## Fact Gate

Extract and check:

- time and date
- location
- person names
- titles and affiliations
- organization names
- 主办 / 联合主办 / 协办 / 支持单位关系
- talk titles, agenda items, product names
- numbers, scale, deadlines, registration links
- quotes and paraphrases
- images, screenshots, permissions

Mark each item:

- `已支撑`: directly supported by supplied material.
- `弱支撑`: implied but not explicit.
- `待确认`: plausible but needs human confirmation.
- `无支撑`: appears invented or too strong.

## Boundary Gate

Pause and mark risk when the draft includes:

- internal meeting notes or private cooperation context
- unconfirmed strategy language from a partner
- sensitive business, education, medical, family, or personal information
- minors, students, clients, or identifiable private individuals
- photos with faces, names, school, address, QR code, phone, or private chat
- jokes, irony, or emotional fragments that may be misread publicly

## Publishing Voice Gate

Ask:

- Does this sound like the publishing account would say it?
- Is the draft accidentally written from a third-party observer view?
- Does it expose editor workflow the reader does not need?
- Does it overstate partner value, strategic meaning, or product maturity?
- Does it repeat the same structure used in nearby articles?

## Commercial Content Gate

For paid or partner content, be stricter:

- Confirm partner names, titles, and product terms.
- Confirm strategic value is represented accurately.
- Separate public facts from internal context.
- Avoid inventing impact claims.
- Keep wording auditable by an editor.

## Output Template

```text
必须改
- [问题] -> [原因] -> [建议]

AI 味在哪里
- 原句：
  为什么像 AI：
  改法：

待确认
- [事实 / 口径 / 授权 / 链接]

可直接替换
- 原句：
  新句：
```

## Stop Rule

If a high-risk fact cannot be checked from supplied material, do not silently rewrite around it. Mark it as `待确认` and tell the user what source would resolve it.
