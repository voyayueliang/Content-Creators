# Risk Signals

Use this reference to decide when to pause instead of drafting.

## Public Boundary Risks

Pause when material includes:

- private chat logs
- internal meeting notes
- unconfirmed partner plans
- unpublished product, revenue, funding, or client information
- personal stories not clearly authorized for publication
- photos with faces, names, schools, addresses, QR codes, phone numbers

## Relationship Risks

Pause when:

- a person may be overinterpreted
- a joke or emotional sentence may be misunderstood in public
- a collaborator's strategic value is being guessed
- a community member, student, client, or participant becomes material without consent

## Fact Risks

Pause when:

- time, place, title, organization, speaker, or link is missing
- source materials disagree
- a claim sounds stronger than the evidence
- AI appears to have filled in a gap

## Voice Risks

Pause when:

- the draft sounds like any account could publish it
- the text explains internal process to readers
- every section has the same rhythm
- the article turns people into examples too quickly
- the draft is fluent but emotionally flat

## Commercial Content Risks

Be stricter for paid or partner content:

- separate public facts from internal context
- confirm partner and sponsor roles
- avoid invented impact claims
- keep a list of items that editor or client must confirm
