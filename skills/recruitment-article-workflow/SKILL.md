# Recruitment Article Workflow

Use this skill when the task is not just to explain a project, but to ask the right people to join, apply, sign up, recommend someone, or start a conversation.

The goal is to make the recruitment ask concrete, honest, and easy to act on. Do not turn weak materials into a generic enthusiastic call. First clarify who is being recruited, why they should care, what they will actually do, what they may gain, what the commitment is, and what must be confirmed before publishing.

## Required Inputs

Ask for or infer:

- recruitment type: hiring, intern, volunteer, fellow, community member, research/interview participant, event attendee, program applicant, partner contributor
- publishing account or organization
- target people
- why this recruitment exists now
- role or participation task
- expected commitment: time, location, duration, workload, output, attendance, application process
- what participants get: pay, learning, network, visibility, access, mentorship, collaboration, or non-monetary value
- requirements and selection criteria
- who should not apply
- application method, deadline, contact, links, form questions, screening process
- public boundaries: salary, personal data, minors, research consent, private relationships, unconfirmed details
- desired tone and platform

If critical facts are missing, produce a recruitment brief and missing-info list before drafting.

## Workflow

1. Classify the recruitment.
   - Hiring: attract candidates for a job or freelance role.
   - Community or fellow: invite people into a project, cohort, storyteller group, open-source/community role, or long-term collaboration.
   - Research or interview: recruit participants, cases, interviewees, or sample recommenders.
   - Event or program: invite signups for a salon, workshop, training camp, course, or closed gathering.

2. Name the real recruitment task.
   - Is the goal applications, referrals, screened signups, sample collection, partner leads, or community activation?
   - What action should the reader take after reading?
   - What would count as a good response?

3. Build the reader portrait.
   - Who is likely to care?
   - What are they already doing or struggling with?
   - What may make them hesitate?
   - What information do they need before acting?

4. Separate offer, bar, and boundary.
   - Offer: what the person gets or participates in.
   - Bar: what kind of person fits and what minimum commitment is expected.
   - Boundary: what is not promised, not suitable, not public, or still to confirm.

5. Choose the draft structure.
   - Hiring article: `why this work matters -> who we are -> what this role does -> who fits -> what we offer -> process and CTA`.
   - Community/fellow recruitment: `why this project exists -> who we are looking for -> what participants will do -> what they can gain -> commitment -> application`.
   - Research/interview recruitment: `research question -> who we want to talk to -> what participation means -> privacy/consent -> how to join or recommend`.
   - Activity/program signup: `why gather now -> who should come -> what will happen -> capacity/screening -> practical info ->报名方式`.

6. Draft in two layers.
   - First output a recruitment brief: task, audience, offer, bar, boundary, missing facts, recommended structure.
   - Only after the brief is confirmed, draft the public article or post.

7. Run final checks before delivery.
   - facts: role, time, location, salary/pay, application method, deadline, contact, link, quota, selection process
   - promises: do not overpromise outcome, network, learning, exposure, job opportunity, or research impact
   - privacy and consent: especially for research/interview participants, minors, students, private communities, and sensitive identity traits
   - voice: remove generic hype, empty enthusiasm, and slogan endings
   - CTA: make the next action obvious and low-friction

## Output Contract

For first pass, use:

1. `招募任务`
2. `适合谁`
3. `读者为什么会关心`
4. `你提供什么`
5. `你期待对方做什么`
6. `筛选标准和不适合人群`
7. `缺失或需确认信息`
8. `建议文章结构`
9. `下一步：补信息 / 写初稿 / 先做表单 / 先确认边界`

For drafting, include:

- title options
-导读 or opening
-正文 draft
-报名 / 申请 / 推荐 CTA
- optional FAQ
- final fact and boundary checklist

## Voice Rules

- Prefer concrete tasks over abstract values.
- Prefer "你会做什么、得到什么、投入什么" over "寻找优秀的你".
- Avoid empty phrases such as "期待优秀的你加入", "一起共创未来", "热爱即可", "机会难得".
- Do not copy corporate recruitment tone unless the publishing account requires it.
- For Moon-style or creator/community recruitment, keep the voice warm, direct, and specific.
- Do not write from an internal organizer perspective only. The reader should understand why this matters to them.

## Safety And Boundary Rules

- Do not invent pay, benefits, quota, deadline, acceptance rate, location, or selection criteria.
- If compensation is unpaid, unclear, or relationship-based, mark it explicitly and recommend confirming contribution boundaries.
- For research participant recruitment, require purpose, consent, privacy treatment, recording/quotation rules, and how data will be used.
- For minors, students, medical, psychological, employment, or vulnerable groups, mark extra consent and privacy risks.
- For private chats, internal strategy, or personal names, do not include details in public copy unless the user confirms permission.
- Send publishable drafts through `$creator-voice-guardian` before final delivery.
